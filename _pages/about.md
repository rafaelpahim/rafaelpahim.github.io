I am Adjunct Professor of Economics at the Federal University of Rio Grande (Brazil) and a PhD candidate in Economics at the Federal University of Rio Grande do Sul (UFRGS), with a Visiting Research period at Colorado State University (2024–2025). I hold both a master's and a bachelor's degree from UFRGS.

My research focuses on the effects of monetary policy on income and functional distribution. I combine a post-Keynesian theoretical background with econometric modeling — mostly difference-in-differences (DiD) and Local Projections. My current work investigates the effects of central bank policy rates on distributional measurements using a long-run panel of countries. I have published in *Feminist Economics*, *the Brazilian Keynesian Review*, and others. I have also presented papers at the *United Nations (UNCTAD)*, *Sorbonne Université (French Association for Political Economy)*, *Roma Tre University (School of Advanced Studies in the Reappraisal of the Surplus Approach)*, *the Levy Institute at Bard College*, *the Monetary Policy and Income Distribution Workshop (University of Toronto)*, *the Eastern Economics Association Conference (Boston)*, and other national and international conferences.
